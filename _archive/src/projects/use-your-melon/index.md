## Overview

A large-scale gesture-based installation at the Georgia Aquarium that challenges guests to a game of echolocation charades while they wait in the queue for the dolphin show.

## Role

Designer and prototyper. Responsible for interaction design, computer vision gesture recognition, and exhibit hardware integration.

## Details

The exhibit uses real-time pose estimation to detect guest gestures, mapping them to dolphin echolocation behaviors. Guests "become" the dolphin — clicking, scanning, and navigating by sound.

The queue environment presented a unique design challenge: no tutorial time, diverse audience ages and abilities, and a noisy, distracting context. The design succeeds through physicality — if you see someone doing it, you immediately understand the interaction.
