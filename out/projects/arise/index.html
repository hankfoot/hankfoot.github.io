OVERVIEW

My Contributions

  • Led UX redesign of the ARISE mobile AR experience across iOS and Android, refreshing UI assets for 5 minigames
  • Conducted user research sessions with child life specialists at partnering hospitals to validate design decisions
  • Created gameplay flow diagrams, wireframes, and high-fidelity mockups in Figma, and implemented final designs in Unity/C#

In a Clamshell 🦀

ARISE is a mobile app that encourages pediatric patients to get up and move by sending them on an immersive undersea adventure. The app uses augmented reality to transform hospital hallways into an interactive ocean world where kids complete quests and collect virtual sea creatures.

I joined the ARISE team as the UX designer in charge of refreshing the app's beta interface. The existing UI was functional but cluttered, with low-contrast text, inconsistent navigation patterns, and visual noise that made it hard for young patients to focus on gameplay.

Objectives

01 — Simplify navigation so young patients can play independently without adult assistance

02 — Increase visual contrast and readability for use in varying hospital lighting conditions

03 — Unify the visual language across all 5 minigames while preserving each game's unique character

2

FINAL DESIGNS

The UI refresh touched every screen in the app. Below are before/after comparisons of the four major sections: Main Menu, Inventory, Quests, and Accounts.

Main Menu

Pre-Refresh
  • • Cluttered navigation with competing visual elements
  • • Low contrast text difficult to read in bright environments
  • • Inconsistent button sizing and placement
Post-Refresh
  • • Simplified navigation hierarchy with clear visual priority
  • • High-contrast accessible color palette
  • • Consistent, touch-friendly button sizing

Inventory

Pre-Refresh
  • • Dense grid layout overwhelming for young users
  • • No clear visual feedback for collected vs. uncollected items
Post-Refresh
  • • Cleaner grid with breathing room between items
  • • Clear visual states for collected, uncollected, and locked creatures

Quests

Pre-Refresh
  • • Quest objectives buried in small text
  • • No progress indication for multi-step quests
Post-Refresh
  • • Large, scannable quest cards with clear objectives
  • • Visual progress bar and step indicators
  • • Added contextual help screen for first-time players

Accounts

Pre-Refresh
  • • Complex account creation flow with too many steps
  • • Avatar selection felt disconnected from the ocean theme
Post-Refresh
  • • Streamlined 3-step account creation
  • • Sea creature avatar selection tied to gameplay progression

PROCESS

01

Gameplay Flow Diagrams

Before touching any visuals, I mapped the entire app's screen-to-screen flow. This revealed redundant navigation paths and dead-end states that were confusing young users. The diagrams became our shared reference for the rest of the project.

02

Research

Note:Due to patient privacy regulations (HIPAA), we were unable to conduct direct usability testing with pediatric patients. Instead, we validated our designs through interviews with child life specialists who work directly with patients daily.

03

Design Workshops

I facilitated design workshops with the SpellBound team to align on visual direction. We explored multiple aesthetic directions before converging on a clean, high-contrast style that maintained the playful ocean theme while improving usability.

Wrapping Up

Working on ARISE was a formative experience — designing for children in hospitals requires a unique blend of playfulness and sensitivity. Every design decision had to account for kids who might be tired, anxious, or in pain.

The refreshed UI shipped in 2021 and is currently in use at over 20 pediatric hospitals. I'm grateful to the child life specialists who gave us their time and insight, and to the SpellBound team for trusting me with this redesign.
