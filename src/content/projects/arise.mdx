## My Contributions

- Led UX redesign of the ARISE mobile AR experience across iOS and Android, refreshing UI assets for 5 minigames
- Conducted user research sessions with child life specialists at partnering hospitals to validate design decisions
- Created gameplay flow diagrams, wireframes, and high-fidelity mockups in Figma, and implemented final designs in Unity/C#

## In a Clamshell 🦀

ARISE is a mobile app that encourages pediatric patients to get up and move by sending them on an immersive undersea adventure. The app uses augmented reality to transform hospital hallways into an interactive ocean world where kids complete quests and collect virtual sea creatures.

I joined the ARISE team as the UX designer in charge of refreshing the app's beta interface. The existing UI was functional but cluttered, with low-contrast text, inconsistent navigation patterns, and visual noise that made it hard for young patients to focus on gameplay.

<ImageBlock data={{ src: "/media/projects/arise/hero/clamshell-overview.png", alt: "ARISE app overview showing the refreshed UI", caption: "Overview of the refreshed ARISE interface", variant: "contained" }} />

## Objectives

01 — Simplify navigation so young patients can play independently without adult assistance

02 — Increase visual contrast and readability for use in varying hospital lighting conditions

03 — Unify the visual language across all 5 minigames while preserving each game's unique character

## Wrapping Up

Working on ARISE was a formative experience — designing for children in hospitals requires a unique blend of playfulness and sensitivity. Every design decision had to account for kids who might be tired, anxious, or in pain.

The refreshed UI shipped in 2021 and is currently in use at over 20 pediatric hospitals. I'm grateful to the child life specialists who gave us their time and insight, and to the SpellBound team for trusting me with this redesign.
